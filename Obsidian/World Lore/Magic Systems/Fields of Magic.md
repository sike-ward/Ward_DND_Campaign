# Fields of Magic

Magic is categorized into conceptual Fields—primal metaphysical divisions that define its nature and limitations. Some Fields are well-known, others remain theoretical or poorly understood.

## Known Fields

### Field of the First
The original shaping force. Its true nature is mysterious—possibly a philosophical construct or a subtle reality-shaping force. Evidence is rare and often debated. Associated with unexplained magical phenomena and belief’s power to alter reality.

### Field of Divine Magic
The metaphysical bridge between mortals and cosmic authority. Power flows from gods, spirits, ideals, or cosmic echoes. Channeled through faith, oaths, or ritual—responsive but not sentient. Reinforces feedback loops of belief and devotion.

### Field of Arcane Magic
The domain of structure, logic, and pattern. Manipulated through study, symbols, and formulae. Highly stable but reactive to misuse. Morally indifferent—favored by scholars and artificers.

### Field of Nature Magic
Primal force of the physical world and its cycles. Accessed via sacred places, spirits, and natural phenomena. Responds to balance and cycles rather than ritual or intellect. Includes phenomena like ley lines.

### Field of Psionic Magic
Born of inner will, thought, and self-identity. Channeled through cognition and mental focus rather than external force. Volatile, often appearing in individuals through trauma, meditation, or contact with psychic realms.

### Field of Dunamancy
Manipulates possibility, probability, and time. Shifts fate and causality. Rare, fragile, and prone to paradox. Emerges from interactions of Arcane, First, and Time fields. Chronurgy (time) and Graviturgy (gravity) are sub-schools.

### Field of Hemocraft
Magic of blood and biological essence. Draws on vitality, ancestry, and sacrifice. Seen as taboo or dangerous. Bypasses divine or arcane structures—manifested through inherited traits or rituals.

### Field of Soul Magic
Deals with memory, essence, and identity. Tied to reincarnation, resurrection, and curses. Difficult to access and study—often appears in times of transformation or death.

### Field of Wild Magic
Embodies chaos and unpredictability—magic in its purest, least controlled form. Associated with fey, liminal spaces, and unstable planar boundaries. Surrender and acceptance are key to channeling this Field.

### Field of Far Magic
Originates from beyond known cosmology—alien and aberrant. Defies natural and metaphysical laws. Associated with madness, mutation, and eldritch forces. Little is known and most interactions are unpredictable or dangerous.
